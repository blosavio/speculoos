Motivation

I wondered if someone could write a spec.alpha-like thing that used plain data structures and predicates. After a couples weeks of morning walks around my neighborhood, I stared to wonder if I could write a spec.alpha-like substance that uses plain data structures and predicates. My brain just wouldn't drop the idea and it seemed like a fun project to learn Clojure. Thus, Speculoos.

Clojure is the niftiest programming language I've encountered. It would be cool if Clojure hung around for 100 years. While much of Clojure is stable and mature, the data specification story seems to be unsettled.The core team has two projects in multiple years of alpha stage, and quite a few open source libraries have been published. For whatever reason, the evolution of a stable answer is taking longer than other parts of the ecosystem. Perhaps I can contribute to the Clojure community by nudging that evolution just a tiny bit. Or maybe there's room for multiple solutions with different approaches.

At this point, my loftiest aspiration is that the core team would notice and be motivated to release some new version of spec.
